A cat is a small, carnivorous mammal of the family Felidae, order Carnivora. It is the only domesticated species in the family Felidae, and was tamed in the Near East around 7500 BC. Cats have supple bodies, long tails, specialized teeth and claws, and finely molded heads. They are also called domestic cats or house cats when kept as indoor pets.

Deer are a family of hoofed ruminants. They have two large and two small hooves on each foot and antlers in the males of most species and in the females of one species. Deer live mainly in forests but may be found in deserts, tundra, and swamps and on high mountainsides.

Elephants are the largest land mammals on earth. There are three different species of elephant: the African Savannah elephant, the African Forest elephant, and the Asian elephant. Elephants have distinctly massive bodies, large ears, and long trunks

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest living cat species. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange fur with a white underside. The tiger is an apex predator that primarily preys on ungulates, such as deer and wild boar

The lion is a large and powerful cat that lives in Africa and Asia. It is known as the king of the jungle because of its majestic mane, strong claws, and sharp teeth. Lions are predatory animals that hunt and eat hoofed animals like wildebeests, zebras, and antelopes. They have a long body, a big head, and a muscular tail that helps them balance when they run or hunt

Squirrels are rodents with strong hind legs and bushy tails. They are found all over the world, except in Australia, where they were introduced by humans. They vary in size, colour, and form, and live in trees or on the ground. They are mostly active during the day or at dusk and dawn, but some are nocturnal

Fox, any of various members of the dog family (Canidae) resembling small to medium-sized bushy-tailed dogs with long fur, pointed ears, and a narrow snout.

Zebras are closely related to domestic horses. They are large single-hoofed ungulates built for speed and long-distance migrations. Zebras typically stand about 120-140 cm (47-55 inches) at the shoulder. Male Grevy's zebras are larger than females; in the plains zebra and the mountain zebra, the sexes are nearly the same size.
